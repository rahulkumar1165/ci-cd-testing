My Thinking Process

1. Identify Root Problems

Focused on actual on-site issues like delays, lack of visibility, and manual workflows instead of surface-level UI problems.

2. Structure Chaos

Converted messy real-world workflows into structured systems with clear relationships (tasks, resources, dependencies).

3. Design for Scale

Ensured every feature works across projects, teams, and future use cases by building reusable patterns.

Schedule Optimizer

Problem: Project timelines were unreliable due to disconnected planning across teams.

Solution: Introduced dependency-based scheduling with clear task relationships.

Impact: Reduced planning delays by ~30%.

Resource Management

Problem: Site managers lacked visibility into labor and material allocation.

Solution: Designed real-time dashboards for tracking and allocation.

Impact: Improved utilization and reduced idle resources.

Daily Reporting

Problem: Manual reporting slowed down decision-making.

Solution: Simplified UX for quick, structured daily updates.

Impact: Increased reporting speed by ~40%.

Document Management

Problem: Documents were scattered across tools and teams.

Solution: Built centralized system with tagging and collaboration.

Impact: Faster document retrieval and better collaboration.

Quality & Snags

Problem: Issues were not tracked systematically.

Solution: Created structured checklist and snag tracking workflows.

Impact: Improved issue resolution speed and accountability.
